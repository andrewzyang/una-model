Simplified view of una_edges.csv (98 canonical edges). ROIs aggregated to the model’s 31 functionally-distinct nodes (from 44 canonical regions); an automated check confirms no inhibitory arrow is hidden by a merge, and full subregion detail (shell/core, D1/D2, PL/IL, DMS/DLS, the six MTL relays, SNc/SNL) stays in each arrow’s tooltip and the arrow-list. Net-effect colouring: green = net-activating, red = net-inhibiting; some green arrows are modulatory/disinhibitory (DA/ACh/NA gating, disinhibition) rather than fast glutamate — see their transmitter label. Diagram 1 Cue (model stage 1) is an integrated cue circuit — perception + interoception in one graph (no channel split), converging on the NAc with the VTA dopamine hub and the habenula / BNST anti-reward brakes · then 2 Motivation (stage 3) · 3 Action (stage 4) · 4 Reward (stage 5) · 5 Consolidation (stage 6 + Repeat). The grey lines crossed with ✕✕✕ mark top-down control shown hypofunctional / absent — the iRISA executive override (4-09 mPFC/vmPFC→NAc; human dlPFC concept) and the IL→shell “stop” / extinction brake (6-02/Repeat-05) fail in addiction (Goldstein & Volkow 2011), leaving bottom-up cue/habit/incentive drive — and the PL→core “go” arm — unopposed.
